I've lived in the United States all my life.
I grew up in a suburb outside of Los Angeles called Chino Hills. I attended kindergarten through high school here.
Growing up, music and art played a large part of my life. I started playing piano when I was seven, and picked up flute not long after. I participated in orchestras and band up to and through high school.
After graduating from high school I didn't go very far to attend college. I moved to Claremont, CA (a twenty minute drive from my hometown) and attended Scripps College.
I graduated in May, 2015 with a B.A. in Computational Neuroscience.
During my time at Scripps, I worked as a Research Assistant for Professor Alison Harris in the Decision Neuroscience Lab at Claremont McKenna College. It was here that I was exposed to electroencephalography and data analysis on brain wave data.

My senior thesis was titled "Time-Frequency Analysis of Neural Oscillations in Multi-Attribute Decision Making".
You can download my thesis here, if you'd like!
I moved to San Francisco soon after graduating from Scripps and began my first job at SOASTA working as a Data Scientist. At SOASTA, we worked with terabytes of real-user monitoring (RUM) data collected from our clients' websites to diagnose, model, and recommend areas on the site to improve.
A few years after I joined the company, Akamai acquired SOASTA. In spring of 2018, I left Akamai and joined conDati.
After living in S.F. for 3 years my s.o. and I decided on a whim to move to Seattle during summer of 2018. We're still living here now, and spend our free time exploring all nature has to offer! I also picked up wheelthrowing and continue to knit, practice yoga, cook, and explore the new city.
In the summer of 2019, we moved to a house in West Seattle. We are loving having a yard and recently got some chickens!
